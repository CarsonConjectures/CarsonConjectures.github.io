// WEAPON EVALUATION — CONFIDENCE & PREDICTION INTERVALS //
REFERENCE: CI & PI BASICS A Confidence Interval (CI) estimates where the population mean is likely to be. Width depends on variance and n.
A Prediction Interval (PI) estimates the range of a single future observation. Width depends mainly on variance.

Key formulas for Uniform(a, b):
Mean: μ = (a + b) / 2
Variance: σ² = (b − a)² / 12
SD: σ = √(σ²)
SE (standard error): SE = σ / √n
2SD CI: x̄ ± 2 · SE
Theory CI: x̄ ± z* · SE
PI: x̄ ± z* · σ · √(1 + 1/n)

Changing variance affects both CI and PI widths.
Changing n affects CI width but not PI width.
Changing μ₀ (null value) affects neither CI nor PI width.

// CENTRAL LIMIT THEOREM — WACKY BLASTER ANALYSIS //
REFERENCE: CENTRAL LIMIT THEOREM (CLT) Due to the Central Limit Theorem, the sum (and mean) of several independent and identically distributed random variables becomes approximately normally distributed as the number of samples increases, regardless of the original distribution shape.

If each shot does damage X with mean μ and variance σ², and you fire n shots:
Sum S = X₁ + X₂ + ... + Xₙ: mean = n·μ, variance = n·σ²
Mean X̄ = S/n: mean = μ, variance = σ²/n

Note: We use z (not t) here because the population SD is known.

The Wacky Blaster does an average of 20 damage per shot with a standard deviation of 5. The damage per shot follows a strange, non-normal distribution — but you are firing 30 shots total, so CLT may apply.
